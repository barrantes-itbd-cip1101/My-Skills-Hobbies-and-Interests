My Memoir.



MY CHILDHOOD YEARS

          I relish the fond memories of my childhood, a time marked by carefree joy and genuine happiness. [1] Those early years were filled with delightful moments spent playing with my cousins and basking in the unconditional love and attention bestowed upon me by my parents and relatives. [2] Being the recipient of such affection made me feel cherished and at times, spoiled in the best possible way. [3]

          One cherished routine was our merienda sessions in the courtyard, where I carried a small maleta containing my favorite snacks, creating a sense of anticipation and excitement. [4] These moments, shared with my Papa, became a source of joy and connection. [5] Our family excursions and adventures allowed me to explore life and nature, fostering a deep appreciation for the world around me. [6]

          Enrolling in a quality school added another layer of enrichment to my childhood, providing opportunities for learning and growth. [7] Reflecting on these experiences, I am filled with a longing to return to a time when innocence prevailed, and the simplicity of just being myself brought unparalleled contentment. [8]

          In those carefree days, there was no need to prove anything, and life was a tapestry of joyous moments that I now hold dear. [9] The nostalgia for those carefree years persists, a testament to the authenticity and purity of childhood happiness. [10] I can confidently state that my childhood is indeed a treasure; I mean, who wouldn't consider theirs as such? [11]

          Moreover, my early years were not confined to a single location, as I used to live in Cavite during my kindergarten days. [12] Our family had a house there, and my grandma, in her doting manner, spoiled me. [13] However, circumstances led us to relocate to our house in Taguig, where my educational journey began from kindergarten onwards. [14]

          During my school years in Taguig, a fascinating opportunity unfolded as I was handpicked by the badminton coach to be a potential varsity player. [15] The coach recognized my skills and wanted me to compete at APSA-TAPAT to represent our school. [16] This was a thrilling prospect, but unfortunately, challenges with the required documents emerged, thwarting my chance to become a varsity player and participate in the competition. [19] Despite this setback, the experience fueled my determination to excel in other aspects of my life, adding another layer to the complex tapestry of my childhood and adolescence. [20]



MY TEENAGE YEARS

          During my teenage years, I underwent a profound awakening, realizing the inherent challenges and occasional disasters that life can present. [1] This period of my life prompted a rapid maturation beyond my chronological age, as I grappled with the complexities of existence. [2] Taking on the responsibility of self-care and self-reliance became paramount, instilling in me a sense of independence that extended beyond societal expectations. [3] Striving to be a positive role model for those in my orbit, I cultivated wisdom that transcended conventional wisdom, navigating the intricacies of real-life situations. [4]

          Reflecting on this phase, I find myself caught between a dichotomy of happiness and weariness, with a sense of trepidation for the impending steps into the next chapter of my life. [5] Despite being on the verge of turning 20, I still consider myself enveloped in the tapestry of adolescence. [6] These formative years were a crucible of learning and unlearning, marked by growth, suffering, loss, and the seizing of opportunities to construct my identity. [7] As I venture into the world, I showcased my abilities, overcoming fears and boldly facing the uncertainties that lay ahead. [8]

          Above all, I discovered the strength to confront challenges independently, with only modest assistance from those in my immediate surroundings. [9] This period stands as the crucible where I truly came to know myself. Moreover, it instilled in me a profound appreciation for resilience, as I learned to navigate life's twists and turns with unwavering determination. [10] The experiences of my teenage years not only equipped me with practical skills but also fueled a sense of purpose and a readiness to embrace the evolving chapters of my journey. [11]

          In addition to these experiences, my journey took an unexpected turn during my high school years. [12]Circumstances led our family to relocate once again, this time to my lola's house on my mother's side. [13] A tragic event in our lives had turned the house we previously inhabited into a source of trauma, making it untenable for us to continue living there. [14]

          This relocation marked a challenging period, especially during my Grade 9 and 10 years. [15] I became exceptionally shy to the point where I barely spoke or participated in class, extracurricular activities, and organizations or clubs. [16] The trauma from our previous living situation cast a shadow over my confidence, inhibiting my ability to engage fully in the school community. [17]

          However, a turning point came when one of my teachers recognized my potential and convinced me to join the Shakespearean Club, also known as the English Club. [18] Initially resistant, I hesitated to audition for the role of emcee for our English Month celebration. [19] The event was significant, held in a large venue, and the idea of being on stage intimidated me. [20] However, with all members required to audition, I summoned the courage to participate. [21] To my surprise, I secured the role of Male emcee. Though challenging, this experience served as a catalyst for boosting my confidence. [22]

          Entering Senior High School (SHS), even amidst the challenges of an online learning environment, I learned to break free from my shell. [23] I actively participated in various activities, winning accolades such as SHS Mr. Mythical Icon 2020 in Mythological Fete and emerging as the champion in the Bible Month Quiz Bee. I embraced the mantra of joining and enjoying, and in doing so, I became a consistent honor student. [24]

          These diverse experiences have woven a rich tapestry of my adolescence, encompassing the struggles, triumphs, and personal growth that have shaped my journey into adulthood. [25] They underline the importance of resilience, self-discovery, and the willingness to embrace opportunities, even in the face of challenges. [26]



MY COLLEGE YEARS

          My college journey has proven to be a challenging yet enlightening chapter in my life, offering profound lessons about both the world around me and my own identity. [1] Throughout my teenage years, I relentlessly pursued excellence, striving to meet the lofty expectations I set for myself in every aspect of life. [2] However, college has taught me that the pursuit of perfection and being at the pinnacle is not always the key to success; survival, authenticity, and personal fulfillment matter just as much. [3]

          The experience has been a delightful revelation, revealing facets of myself that I thought I already knew. [4] The diverse array of people I've encountered, from friends and colleagues to seemingly random strangers, has expanded my understanding of human connections. [5] It has made me question the notion that mediocrity is inherently wrong, prompting the realization that one doesn't always need to be the best at everything. Simply being oneself and doing things with genuine enjoyment can be enough. [6]

          College has underscored the importance of embracing life for the joy it brings rather than turning everything into a competition. [7] It's acceptable to engage in activities purely for the pleasure they bring, without the pressure of excelling or outperforming others. [8] The revelation that one doesn't need an exhaustive knowledge of history or any subject matter to exist comfortably is liberating. [9]

          While I still maintain a degree of concern for grades and seek academic validation, there's a newfound acceptance that perfection is not the sole measure of success. [10] Striking a balance between academic conscientiousness and the pursuit of personal happiness has become a defining aspect of my college experience, with a lighthearted acknowledgment that aiming for 50% is perfectly okay. [11]

          Moreover, my college journey has been enriched by my active involvement in various organizations. [12] During my first year, I became a member of COMSSO. Over time, my commitment and passion for the organization grew, leading me to take on a more significant role. [13] Currently, I proudly serve as the Vice President for Internal Affairs of COMSSO, where I am responsible for handling seminars, academic matters, papers, and other internal affairs. [14]

          Being part of COMSSO has become more than just a commitment; it is a source of joy and fulfillment. [15] I find myself immersed in the intricacies of organizing seminars, addressing academic concerns, and collaborating with fellow members to create a vibrant and supportive community. [16] This role not only allows me to contribute meaningfully to the organization but also provides a platform for personal and professional growth. [17] Additionally, I am currently exploring the possibility of extending my involvement in campus organizations. [18] Recently, I filled out a form to join the UE Red Cross Youth Council. [19]

          In embracing this responsibility, I've discovered a newfound love for what I do and thoroughly enjoy the challenges and opportunities it brings. [21] It has reinforced the idea that success is not solely measured by individual achievements but is also derived from collective efforts and the positive impact we make within our community. [22] Through my role in COMSSO, I've learned to appreciate the significance of balance, not only in academics but also in fostering a sense of belonging, camaraderie, and shared purpose within the college community.
